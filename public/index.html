What is SproutEd?

SproutEd started as a way to rethink food education and combat food waste by reconnecting students with the source of their food. Itâ€™s a tool to make healthy eating habits more approachable, gardening more rewarding, and sustainable practices easier.

As a hands-on plant kit and STEM challenge designed for students ages 8+, SproutEd is built for both those with a green thumb and others who've never touched a plant before.

By connecting students with the growing process and using real-time sensor data, SproutEd helps teach better food habits, spark curiosity about sustainability, and build a stronger relationship between young people and the food systems that support them.

How Does it Work?

Each SproutEd kit includes everything you need to start growing your own plant and to compete on a live leaderboard. Sensor data from the plant updates in real-time, helping students stay engaged and motivated to care for their plant.

1 Unpack your kit and connect your sensor box.
2 Track moisture, sunlight, and growth progress.
3 Compete with others while learning about food systems.

Why It Matters

Food waste is a major issue in the U.S. and particularly impacts young people. By teaching food literacy through gardening, SproutEd hopes to cultivate habits that reduce waste and promote healthier food practices early in life.
